Beaumont Park Huddersfield

Beaumont Park is a Victorian-era public park located on the southern edge of Huddersfield in West Yorkshire. Opened in 1883, it is known for its dramatic stone terraces, woodland paths, and scenic views over Colne Valley.

Kirkstall Abbey

Kirkstall Abbey is a historic 12th-century Cistercian monastery located on the banks of the River Aire in Leeds. Now a well-preserved ruin, it is one of the city’s most important heritage sites, known for its impressive Gothic architecture, scenic parkland setting, and peaceful riverside walks.

Mappleton Beach

The wide sandy beach located in front of the East Riding village of Mappleton is ideal for dog walking. This section of North Sea coastline is known as Holderness and is situated around 3 miles (5 km) south of the seaside resort of Hornsea.
